# Design Context

## Users
**Who**: Players of Torchlight Infinite (anime-styled high fantasy ARPG) who farm maps for currency and valuable items. They're efficiency-focused, tracking drops and income per minute to optimize their farming routes.

**Context**: Running in the background during active gameplay sessions. Users glance at it between maps or during downtime to check stats. Power users export data to Excel for deeper analysis.

**Job to be Done**: Track real-time item drops and prices, calculate income efficiency, and provide clear statistics to optimize farming strategies.

## Brand Personality
**Voice**: Precise • Confident • Sharp

**Tone**: This is a serious tool for serious players. Not playful or whimsical—it's about data, efficiency, and optimization. But "serious" doesn't mean boring. Think tactical precision with visual impact.

**Emotional Goal**: Users should feel **in control** and **confident** in their farming decisions. The UI should inspire focus and clarity, making complex data instantly readable.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Visual Tone**: Dark, precise, distinctive

**Core Direction**: Dark mode with an **unusual color palette** that avoids AI-generated tells (no cyan/purple gradients, no glassmorphism everywhere, no rounded rectangles with colored left borders). Focus on:
- **Sharp, distinctive typography** that makes numbers and data pop
- **Memorable motion design** for drop events and statistics updates
- Clean data hierarchy that emphasizes efficiency metrics
- High contrast for quick scanning during gameplay

**Anti-References**:
- Generic dark mode dashboards with purple/blue gradients
- Overuse of blur effects and glass cards
- Rounded corners everywhere with no visual rhythm
- Sparklines and decorative charts that add no value

**Color Strategy**: Dark background with an unexpected accent palette that fits the anime high fantasy vibe:
- Amber/gold (#f59e0b) for currency and primary actions (warm, treasure feeling)
- Sharp cyan (#06b6d4) for highlights and accents (energy, magic)
- Bright green (#22c55e) for positive values with subtle glow
- Deep reds for warnings/costs
- Deep blacks (#0a0a0f) for dramatic contrast
- No purple/violet (overused in gaming UIs, replaced with gold/cyan)

## Design Principles

### 1. Data First, Decoration Never
Every visual element serves the data. No decorative cards, no unnecessary containers, no visual noise. If it doesn't help users read stats faster, it doesn't belong.

### 2. Typography as Statement
Use bold, distinctive font choices that make this tracker instantly recognizable. Numbers are the star—they deserve display-quality type treatment. Create hierarchy through weight, size, and spacing, not color alone.

### 3. Motion with Impact
When items drop, users should *feel* it. When numbers update, they should draw the eye. Use motion purposefully for state changes and important events. Spring physics over linear easing. Quick but satisfying.

### 4. Unusual, Not Chaotic
Break conventions intentionally. Asymmetric layouts, unexpected spacing, creative type scaling. But maintain clear visual logic and information hierarchy. Distinctive doesn't mean confusing.

### 5. Built for Glanceability
Users are actively gaming—they need to scan stats in 2 seconds. Use size, weight, and position to create instant visual hierarchy. Most important metrics (income/min, current value) should dominate the space.
